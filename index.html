Sedentarization of Tibetan Nomads

Sedentarization efforts have been part of the Chinese policy on the Tibetan grassland since the 1960s. Although primary goal is to improve ecosystems and the socio-economic situation of nomadic households, the programs do not focus enough on how ex-nomads cope with new lifestyles in urban settlements.

Learn More

As part of the Great Western Development Strategy, Tibetan nomadic communities have been forced to adjust their lifestyles and become sedentary. The nuances and consequences of this development must be understood by any scholar of Tibetan history and culture.

Western Development

In 1999, the Central Committee of the Communist Party of China formally proposed the implementation of the Western Development Strategy (西部大开发战略)
